# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Adult children and young parents in dispersed families, beginning with Indian and diaspora families around Diwali, birthdays, reunions, new babies, and personal milestones. They want to understand what life felt like for a parent or grandparent at the same age they are now. Invited relatives may be older or non-technical, so contributing must not require an account or a long form.

## Product Purpose

Recollection is an intergenerational time machine. A person meets a parent or grandparent at the same age through one original photo, one honest question, and a clearly marked visual reconstruction of the world around that memory. Success is not an archive entry: it is a living person understanding their own life differently after hearing what a loved one felt at the same age.

## Brand Personality

Luminous, intimate, electric. It should feel like looking through a time portal in a dark room full of festival light, not like an archive, genealogy site, or generic AI tool.

## Anti-references

- Beige scrapbook nostalgia, family-tree databases, and dashboard card grids
- Generic AI chat, prompt boxes, public social feeds, likes, or streaks
- Photorealistic child-face animation, voice cloning, or claims to restore the past
- Cold compliance interfaces that turn family stories into audits

## Design Principles

1. Start with the age bridge: “They were my age once. What did life feel like?”
2. One photo, one question, one human response.
3. Let people and artifacts lead; keep AI backstage.
4. Distinguish family source material from cinematic reconstruction in everyday language.
5. Make the invited relative’s path simpler than opening a messaging app.
6. Treat privacy and deletion as acts of care, not legal footnotes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

High-contrast text, keyboard-operable controls, captions and transcripts for all audio, no autoplaying sound, reduced-motion support, and large tap targets. Preserve original-language audio alongside translations rather than replacing it.
